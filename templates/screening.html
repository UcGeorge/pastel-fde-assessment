PEP & Sanctions Screening

Screen individuals and entities against global watchlists. This tool cross-references two distinct databases — PEP (Politically Exposed Persons) and international Sanctions — returning match confidence scores for any hits found.

What is a PEP?

A Politically Exposed Person is an individual in a prominent public function (heads of state, senior politicians, senior executives of state-owned enterprises). PEPs carry elevated financial crime risk due to their position and potential exposure to bribery or corruption.

What are Sanctions?

Sanctions lists (OFAC, UN, EU, etc.) identify individuals and entities prohibited from financial transactions. Transacting with a sanctioned party constitutes a serious compliance violation and may result in heavy regulatory penalties.
